Four years of learning, failing, growing — and finding out who I really am inside a classroom and beyond it.

Chapter 01

Entering an
Unfamiliar World

2025

Starting university in Information Technology was both exciting and overwhelming. I entered my first semester with almost no background or technical knowledge in IT. Programming, databases, and development concepts felt completely unfamiliar, while many people around me already seemed to understand the basics. At first, it was difficult to keep up, and there were many moments where I felt frustrated and left behind. Even so, giving up was never an option for me. I spent extra time relearning materials outside the classroom and trying to understand concepts step by step. The first semester taught me that growth in technology is not about instantly being good at everything, but about having the patience and consistency to keep learning even when the process feels difficult.

Chapter 02

Learing beyond
The classroom

2026

During the transition between the first and second semester, I started taking learning more seriously. I spent extra time studying independently, watching tutorials, practicing small projects, and revisiting concepts that I still struggled to understand. Slowly, the frustration that once felt overwhelming turned into motivation to keep improving little by little. At the same time, I also started becoming more active outside academics to avoid burnout from constantly chasing technical subjects. I joined a content creation competition held by the university and unexpectedly won 1st place with my team. That experience became a huge motivation boost and reminded me that growth is not only about academics, but also about creativity, confidence, and trying new opportunities.

Chapter 03

Building
New Skills

2026

By the second semester, I finally started feeling more comfortable in the IT environment. The subjects still challenged me, but I no longer felt as lost as I did in the beginning. I had started adapting to the learning pace and understanding how different technical concepts connected with each other. Throughout this semester, I gained many new skills and experiences — from learning HTML and CSS, understanding basic databases, to exploring the fundamentals of web development. Even though my skills are still at a beginner level, seeing my own progress has been incredibly rewarding and makes me excited to continue exploring the world of technology in the future.
